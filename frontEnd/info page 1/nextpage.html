At home:

  • Install Smart Devices: Smart locks, video doorbells, and motion-detection cameras can provide real-time alerts and control.
  • Use Peepholes or Cameras: Always check who is at the door before opening it. Use a peephole, doorbell camera, or intercom.
  • Keep Outdoor Areas Well-Lit: Ensure that entryways, driveways, and dark corners around your home are well-lit.
  • Use Curtains or Blinds: Prevent outsiders from looking into your home by using curtains, blinds, or frosted glass.
  • Know Your Neighbors: Establish a good relationship with your neighbors. They can help monitor your home and assist in emergencies.

At shopping:

  • Visit shopping areas during daylight hours or in well-lit and crowded places.
  • Let someone know your plans, including where you're going and when you expect to return.
  • RideCarry a personal safety device, such as a whistle, pepper spray, or an alarm.
  • RideIf you feel uncomfortable, seek help from security personnel or store employees.
  • RideBe cautious with strangers who approach you with unsolicited offers or conversations.

At travelling:

  • Book accommodations in safe, well-reviewed locations.
  • Social Media:
    Avoid posting real-time updates of your location.
    Share travel photos only after leaving a place.
  • Transportation Safety:
    Avoid traveling alone late at night.
    Sit in the back seat of taxis and use apps like GPS to ensure you’re on the correct route.

At parties:

  • Avoid Leaving Alone:
    Coordinate your departure with trusted friends or call a reliable ride service.
    Avoid walking home alone, especially late at night.
  • Check Transportation Details:
    Verify the identity of ride-share drivers before getting in the car.
    Share your ride details with someone you trust.
  • Watch Your Drinks:
    Always keep your drink in sight.
    Don’t accept drinks from strangers or leave your drink unattended.

Out Alone:

  • Plan Your Route: Stick to well-lit, populated areas, and know your way before heading out. Use navigation apps if needed.
  • Keep Emergency Numbers Handy: Have local emergency contacts (e.g., police, a trusted friend) saved in your phone for quick access.
  • Know Safe Spots:
    Identify police stations, hospitals, or well-known establishments along your route as places to seek help if needed.
  • Keep Phone Settings Secure: Enable features like "Emergency SOS" and location sharing for emergencies.

In Car:

  • Stay Alert:
    Be cautious of suspicious vehicles or individuals following you.
    Avoid stopping for strangers or unknown vehicles signaling for help—call the police if necessary.
  • Be Vigilant:
    Look around before entering your car.
    Check the backseat and under the car for any suspicious activity.
  • Choose Safe Parking:
    Park in well-lit, busy areas. Avoid secluded spots.
    Reverse park for a quicker exit.
